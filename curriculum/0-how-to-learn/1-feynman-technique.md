# How to Learn: The Feynman Technique

Before we dive into RAG, embeddings, and AI agents, we need to talk about **how** you're going to learn this material.

This isn't a typical course where you passively watch videos and hope things stick. You're going to actively teach what you learn—because that's how real understanding happens.

---

## Why You Record Videos Each Week

Every week, you'll record a short video explaining a concept you learned. This isn't busywork. It's based on the **Feynman Technique**, named after the Nobel Prize-winning physicist Richard Feynman.

Here's the idea:

> **If you can't explain something simply, you don't understand it well enough.**

Feynman was famous for being able to explain complex physics concepts to anyone—not because he dumbed things down, but because he truly understood the fundamentals.

---

## The Feynman Technique in 4 Steps

1. **Study the concept** — Learn it like you normally would
2. **Teach it to a child** — Explain it in simple terms, no jargon
3. **Identify gaps** — Where did you struggle to explain? That's where your understanding is weak
4. **Review and simplify** — Go back, fill the gaps, and try again

Your weekly video is step 2. When you hit a wall trying to explain something, that's step 3 showing you exactly where to focus.

## Break Things. Extend Things. Rewrite Things.

The codebase you're working with is **yours to experiment with**.

Don't just follow along. We encourage you to:

- **Break it** — See what happens when you remove a piece. Understand why it fails.
- **Extend it** — Add a feature. Try a different embedding model. Integrate another data source.
- **Rewrite it** — Don't like how something is structured? Refactor it your way.

The goal isn't to complete exercises—it's to deeply understand how RAG applications work so you can build your own from scratch.
