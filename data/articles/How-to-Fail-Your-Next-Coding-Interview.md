# How to Fail Your Next Coding Interview

---

How to Fail Your Next Coding Interview

High salary be damned! You’re over it. LeetCode. AlgoExpert. Interview Kickstart. Suck a lemon. 🍋

You peruse your LinkedIn inbox, scouring over the dozens of unread messages from recruiters. You click on a semi-recent one. “I’ll take it.”

The recruiter replies immediately. Your interview for Front End Developer at BurBur.io, a popular startup with a valuation of 1B is next week.

“It’s a burrito finding app” the recruiter types. “Like Uber… for burritos”.

“Mmmmm” you type back. “Burritos.” 🌯

Burritos indeed.

You’re set on failing this interview to stick it to the man by wasting the valuable time of their overly paid engineers.

You plan on failing in just a few easy steps ->

BurBur.io is a popular company and Glassdoor would certainly give you some insight into past interviews. It probably even has some of the questions and answers verbatim! Absolutely not going to check that out.

Blind.com — it’s like Glassdoor but for tech… and with a lot of uncensored tech bros bragging about their TC (total comp). If you wanted some raw, uncut advice on the interview process you might go there — maybe even reach out to a current employee and ask what to expect. You delete the app. Bwahahahaha.

This is a Front End focused role so you fire up LeetCode and focus on graph, tree and obscure dynamic programming problems. You’re gonna really flub that question on the box-model now.

You know one of the most common challenges in a FE interview for a ReactJS role is to create a component that fetches some data and displays it. You do your best to forget this tidbit of knowledge and if you really need to, you’ll just look up simple concepts like how to use useState and useEffect

You can’t think of a complex problem you solved, a co-worker you had a disagreement with or a question you want to ask to your interviewer. Perfect. Awkward silence for the win!

You’re fully unprepared for the interview now. The day approaches and you click on the Zoom link to begin the hours-long coding challenge.

BurBur.io has recently switched their coding interviews to mirror FAANG type companies. You’re asked a question that has nothing to do with your skillset or the type of work you’ll be doing!

Levenshtein distance.

A*.

Rats. You’re actually passing the challenges.

No one asks about your past experiences. No questions on ReactJS despite this being a ReactJS focused role. Box model? Yeah right.

Congratulations. You passed the interview!

BurBur.io gives you a competitive package. 300k and unlimited burritos. You’ve become everything you hate. BurBur.io goes public months later and tanks in typical Silicon Valley fashion.

You’re pleased and relieved. You finish your burrito. Back to LinkedIn. You’re gonna succeed at failing this time… if it’s the last thing you do.

If you actually want to smash your next interview and understand JS, grab a few minutes with me to brainstorm on your strategy and dig in to what’s holding you back.
