# Yes, Your Bootcamp Is Lying To You About Their Job Placement Rate

---

Yes, Your Bootcamp Is Lying To You About Their Job Placement Rate

Are you really surprised by this?

Most recently, Lambda School was publicly shamed for making up employment statistics about their graduates.

Lambda School placed only 30% of its 2020 graduates in qualifying jobs during the first half of 2020. This figure is in stark contrast to the 74% placement rate it advertised for its 2019 graduate

https://www.classcentral.com/report/lambda-school-placement-rate/

Now, I’ve worked at a few bootcamps over the years, but you don’t need to have worked at one of these schools to understand the challenges that await recent graduates in the job market. Been on LinkedIn lately? How many articles will you read today from recent grads that haven’t been able to find a job in 3 months. 6 Months. 1 year! Or who have completely given up and gone back to their former career. It can be demoralizing to read through these stories as a developer on the job hunt.

I don’t want this to be all doom and gloom, however.

Yes, your bootcamp is likely using creative math to inflate their job placement numbers or outright lying like Lambda School.

We are humans at the end of the day and there are too many external factors that can affect your personal job search to ever guarantee that you will land a job as a software developer in some finite amount of time.

Maybe you barely graduated from your program. Maybe you’re only applying to FAANG (tc or gtfo). Perhaps you just aren’t in a rush to get hired and dragging your feet. Maybe it’s just a bad time of year… many companies wait to hire at certain times of the year.

There are too many reasons why you, specifically, may not be getting hired.

Should you give up on your dreams of becoming a developer and eschew all bootcamp programs? No, you fool.

What you should focus on are the foundational skills that will ensure you are prepared when you meet the right opportunity. The worst mistake I’ve seen students I’ve taught make is to immediately stop coding once their bootcamp ends. Their Githhub history takes a sharp decline the moment guided instruction ends.

There are really only 2 problems that recent bootcamp graduates encounter:

Not getting interviews

Getting interviews and not passing them

Luckily, these are solvable problems. Each requires a different skillset but they are far from insurmountable.

Don’t get bogged down in the sob stories littering LinkedIn or the fact that your bootcamp program may not be forthcoming in their actual job placement numbers. Focus on getting 500 connections on LinkedIn. A/B test your resume and keep a side project going to keep your coding skills sharp. You may not be able to control the length of your search but you can certainly make sure you are discoverable and have the right skills to get hired when the opportunity arises.
