The pipeline you're building

Songs flow left to right. Each stage pulls from a queue, does its work, and pushes to the next queue — no stage ever calls another directly. Run it, then turn on Chaos mode and watch failures get retried without the rest of the pipeline noticing. Then scale the download workers and watch its backlog drain.

Download workers:
Why queues matter: a failed download goes back on the queue and is retried — resolution and scraping never notice. And because each stage has its own workers, you can scale the slow, heavy stage (downloads) without touching the fast ones. That isolation is the entire argument for this architecture.
