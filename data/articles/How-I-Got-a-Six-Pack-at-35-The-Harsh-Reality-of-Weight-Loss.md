# How I Got a Six Pack at 35: The Harsh Reality of Weight Loss

---

How I Got a Six Pack at 35: The Harsh Reality of Weight Loss

You want to lose weight. For health reasons. To look good shirtless. To show your ex what they’re missing. I get it. No judgement.

After a solid decade of drinking, partying and generally eating like an asshole, I finally got sober and got my life together. Dropping the bottle was hard. Dropping the 30 pounds I had put on over the years was maybe harder.

I started exercising once I stopped drinking but nearly all of the results I got happened in the last 18 months.

Unfortunately, there is so much “information” out there about dieting and weight loss that it’s easy to get confused or led down the wrong path.

You have good intentions but poor implementation.

Extreme diets aren’t sustainable

There are no magic foods or times of day which you should eat

You’re eating more than you think

You’re making it too complicated

It’s actually pretty simple

It is certainly NOT easy.

Here’s how I did it:

Step 1: Know Your TDEE

Total Daily Energy Expenditure. Basically, how many calories do you burn per day doing you. https://tdeecalculator.net/

Use the calculator in the link above to determine your TDEE. (HINT: choose ‘Sedentary’ for Activity Level).

Great. Now you have a baseline for how many calories you need a day.

Step 2: Measure What You Eat

99% of us eat more than we think.

Now that you know the number of calories needed to sit on your ass all day, we have some basic math to do:

Eat less calories than used for your TDEE.

Buy a digital food measuring scale. They’re not expensive you cheap bastard. No excuses. You cannot eyeball the amount of rice you’re stuffing down your face.

Track every goddamn thing you eat into MyFitnessPal. It’s also free.

Step 3: Plan Out Your Staple Meals

I don’t meal prep.

Maybe you don’t want to either.

Pick a couple staple meals that fit your calorie goals.

Perhaps you like sweets like me so make sure to incorporate those into your calorie budget. Be realistic!

Honestly, it doesn’t really matter where your calories come from as far as weight loss is concerned.

You can lose weight eating nothing but twinkies as long as you are eating less than the number of calories needed to maintain your current body weight.

Don’t do that of course.

Some easy to make, low calorie foods that I use for my daily meals:

Egg whites

Ground turkey (99% fat free)

Zero Pop popcorn

Chocolate rice cakes

Chicken breast

Quest Protein bars

Low calorie mozzarella

Remember, measuring is key!

I buy foods that I can easily measure like cartons of egg whites and ground turkey.

I use measuring cups for my cheese and popcorn… yeah, I know.

Eating out can be a bit of a guessing game but MyFitnessPal has a shocking amount of data for restaurants. Don’t be lazy. Also, maybe eat out less.

Step 4: Weigh Yourself Everyday. Every. Day.

Your weight will fluctuate. Sometimes by a lot.

This is why it’s so important to weigh yourself everyday. You want a downward trend but don’t freak out if there are spikes here and there. That’s normal.

Here’s what my chart looks like:

Always weigh yourself in the morning when you are at your lightest. This is both for your own mental health and to keep an accurate record of your progress.

Don’t be shocked when you go up 5 pounds after binging on salty foods or after a weekend drowning your sorrows at an all-you-can-eat buffet.

On top of weighing yourself, take a revealing picture of yourself every Friday. A nice spicy pic for just your eyes.

The number of pounds you’ve dropped is not always the most accurate reflection of your progress.

Do you look better without clothes? Progress.

Step 5: Know Weight Loss Math.

You know your TDEE.

You are tracking calories.

I hope you are exercising.

So how fast will those rolls disappear?

Depends.

It takes roughly 3500 calories to add a pound. So work backwards.

Can you decrease 500 calories a day from your maintenance calories to be in a 3500 calorie deficit?

I’d say that’s aggressive but possible.

You have a few options:

Eat roughly the same and burn more calories from working out

Eat less and don’t work out

Eat a little less AND workout (the best way)

At the end of the day, you must re-calibrate based on your weight goals.

Not losing weight for 2 weeks? Lower your calories by 200 a day or exercise more.

As long as you are on a downward trend, you are doing great.

Some additional food math you might find useful:

4 Calories per gram in carbs

4 calories per gram in proteins

9 calories per gram in fats

You may notice fats have significantly more calories than proteins and carbs which means if you want to eat more then stay away from high fat foods.

Conclusion: Don’t Give Up

Most people won’t follow the steps I’ve outlined above. They will make every excuse why they can’t. They have a very unique situation which prevents them from weighing food or tracking what they eat. They simply don’t have the time!!!

I know those people. I was a member of their group for quite a while.

Don’t be those people.
